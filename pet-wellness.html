üåø Essential Oils & Pets: Safety First

Essential oils are powerful plant compounds ‚Äî and while some can be supportive, many are unsafe or even toxic for pets, especially cats. Cats lack certain liver enzymes needed to process concentrated plant oils, making them particularly sensitive.

Generally considered safer options (when properly diluted and used cautiously):

  • Frankincense
  • Lavender (true lavender, not blends)
  • Copaiba
  • Helichrysum

Common oils to avoid around pets:

  • Tea tree (melaleuca)
  • Eucalyptus
  • Peppermint
  • Clove
  • Cinnamon
  • Citrus oils

Essential oils should never be applied directly to pets, diffused in enclosed spaces, or used without proper education. Less is more ‚Äî and when in doubt, skip them entirely.
